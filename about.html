ABOUT

International Student Must Item is an archive of objects brought from home countries to Canada. Each item holds memory, identity, and adaptation. Through interviews, this archive documents personal transitions and shows how students adjust to a new environment using familiar things.

The archive includes items such as food, ingredients, clothing, and small everyday objects. These items may look simple, but they carry personal meaning. They are connected to daily habits, culture, and comfort. For many students, these objects help them feel more stable and less disconnected in a new place.

The project looks at how these items are used in daily life. A food item or a small object can help recreate routines from home. At the same time, their meaning can change when they are used in a different country. Students may use them in new ways or combine them with new habits.

By collecting these items and stories, the archive shows both shared and different experiences among students. It highlights how people keep parts of their identity while also adjusting to a new environment. In the end, International Student Must Item presents these objects as part of real lived experience. It shows how students bring pieces of home with them and how these pieces help them build a sense of belonging in Canada.
